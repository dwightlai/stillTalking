# Adult child living at home article design

## Editorial goal

Publish a practical, research-informed guide for American parents whose adult child lives at home. The article should replace shame and vague expectations with a clear adult-to-adult household agreement.

## Reader question

How can parents set fair rules for an adult child living at home without treating the child like a teenager or allowing resentment to build?

## Position and scope

- Living with parents is not evidence of personal failure.
- The central problem is usually ambiguity about authority, privacy, money, labor, guests, and duration.
- Parents retain authority over limits that protect their home and finances.
- Adult children retain authority over their body, relationships, career, schedule, and private information.
- A review date is preferable to either an unspoken permanent arrangement or a surprise move-out ultimatum.
- The article will not diagnose mental illness, prescribe legal terms, or assume every household should charge rent.

- Target length: 1,100–1,400 words

## Article structure

1. Open with a clearly recognizable shared-home conflict and identify it as composite.
2. Normalize the housing context with current Census and Pew findings.
3. Define the difference between household rules and parental control.
4. Explain how to agree on rent, utilities, food, and savings without auditing unrelated spending.
5. Assign concrete household labor rather than relying on “help out more.”
6. Set privacy and guest expectations that apply to adults.
7. Replace a vague move-out expectation with a scheduled review and measurable next steps.
8. Provide a natural conversation script and a short written-agreement checklist.
9. End with one concrete next action and relevant internal links.

## Evidence

- U.S. Census Bureau, `Living Arrangements Varied Across Age Groups`.
- Pew Research Center, `Parents, Young Adult Children and the Transition to Adulthood`.

Only claims directly supported by these sources will use figures. The article will distinguish survey findings from editorial interpretation.
