# Still Talking SEO Operations Checklist

This checklist covers work that must happen in Google Search Console or through external outreach. It does not indicate that any external action has already been performed.

## Initial setup

- Add and verify the production domain property in Google Search Console.
- Submit `https://stilltalkingfamily.com/sitemap.xml`.
- Inspect the homepage, `/library`, all six topic pages, and several representative articles.
- Record the initial counts for valid indexed pages, excluded pages, crawled but not indexed pages, canonical conflicts, and soft 404s.
- Inspect a filtered Library URL and confirm Google can see its `noindex` directive and `/library` canonical.

## Weekly

- Review new indexing errors, 404s, server errors, and sitemap processing failures.
- Check that published articles and topic pages remain eligible for indexing.
- Confirm filtered Library URLs trend toward “Excluded by noindex.”
- Investigate sudden drops in impressions or indexed pages before changing content.

## Monthly

- Record total impressions, clicks, CTR, and average position.
- Export top queries and top pages for comparison with the prior month.
- Review queries with growing impressions but weak CTR, especially positions 4–15.
- For a candidate title change, confirm the page matches the query intent before rewriting metadata.
- After a title change, observe results for three to four weeks before changing it again unless the title is inaccurate.
- For each priority query, switch to the Pages report and check whether several pages alternate for the same term.
- If cannibalization appears, choose one primary page, narrow supporting pages to long-tail intents, and strengthen contextual links to the primary page.
- Check that topic pages receive more internal links than filtered Library URLs.

## Quarterly

- Compare growth across Communication, Boundaries, Money and Support, Independence, Family Relationships, and Conflict and Repair.
- Identify thin topics that need better coverage rather than more near-duplicate pages.
- Review older articles for changed sources, broken links, dated examples, and meaningful update opportunities.
- Decide whether overlapping articles should be differentiated, consolidated, or redirected.
- Review earned backlinks and referral traffic to the conversation-script resource.

## Outreach prerequisites

- Promote a resource only after its HTML page is complete, sources and claims are checked, and it works without an email gate.
- Prioritize family therapists, counseling organizations, aging and parenting publications, university family-research programs, newsletters, and journalists whose audiences genuinely benefit.
- Describe the resource accurately. Do not claim clinical validation, guaranteed outcomes, or expert review that has not occurred.
- Track the page contacted, contact date, resource shared, response, resulting link, and follow-up status.
